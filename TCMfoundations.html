A TCM reading list for aspiring practitioners.

Traditional Chinese Medicine is a qualitative science born out of the observational lens of Taoism and refined by the brutalism of Mao's cultural revolution. Its scope of value is needlessly minimized in the west and what remains is lauded by its beneficiaries and dismissed by its critics. Nevertheless, I remain optimistic that TCM will continue its slow march towards broader acceptance and, more importantly, broader implementation across western society.

TCM is not easy to learn. And western students suffer from various absenses in their learning process. Among these deficiences can be counted the irregular educational standards set by schools, no formalised apprenticeship system for new graduates, and the cultural gap between China and the English speaking west rendering fundamental concepts inadequately translated and thus, diluted of meaning. A simple test of any student's knowledge is to ask for a definition of qi. The answer of energy is a failing grade.

So, I submit this reading list for aspirants, students and curious readers alike. Once these texts are known inside out, one can tentatively, and under good guidance, endevour to treat the 10,000 things. Good luck!

Chinese Herbal Medicine: Materia Medica

Indispensible cornerstone for the English speaking student of Chinese Medicine Herbology. Herbs grouped by function, with great summaries and comparisons. A book I have carried with me around the world.

Acupuncture: A Comprehensive Text

Learn your acupoints here. Just memorize it. Yes, all of it.

Chinese Herbal Medicine: Formulas & Strategies

A guide to applying your differentiated diagnosis to specific herbal formulas. Requires endless study to absorb what is a giant body of work. I try to read one chapter a month in a constant process of review.

Healing With Whole Foods:
Asian Traditions and Modern Nutrition

TCM dietetics are poorly understood in the west. Paul Pitchford has done a great job of easing his readers into these concepts. A book that gathers no dust.

The Foundations of Chinese Medicine: A Comprehensive Text

Maciocia recently passed away but has left English speaking students this simple guide to TCM foundations. Another one to memorize.

The Root of Chinese Qigong—Secrets for Health, Longevity, and Enlightenment

This book provides some basic instruction without complexity or flakiness. Therapeutic exercise is a skill to acquire. Learn to move qi and blood.

Traditional Chinese Medicine Cupping Therapy

Everything you need to know about cupping. A lot more to it than bruises on celebrities' backs. Buy it.

Outline Guide to Chinese Herbal Patent Medicines

Dr. Naeser demystifies the cluttered offerings of all Chinatown herb shops for her readers and leaves Han shopkeeps bemused by their eager 鬼佬 customers.

Practical Therapeutics of Traditional Chinese Medicine

Exemplifies the methodology of order found in TCM therapeutics. The gold standard. I'm lucky to have studied under Warren Fischer.
