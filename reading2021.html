Personal Reading List 2021

Here's my reading list from 2021. By no means exhaustive, it captures the main standouts of 2021. I tend to switch between multiple texts at any one time. A constant re-reader of books and their specific chapters or paragraphs or lines; it slow burns my little grey cells in just the right way.

A History of the Federal Reserve, Volume 1: 1913-1951

Part of the equities and options reading I undertook in the beginning of the year, this book charts the most powerful and successful going concern in the world. Still working my way through it.

A Record of Buddhistic kingdoms: being an account by the Chinese monk Fa-hsien of travels in India and Ceylon

Mad history from a different era and a world almost unrecognizable today. I'm endlessly fascinated by the travelling monk meta, and even more so from the time long before motor vehicles and modernity.

Violence and Serenity: Late Buddhist Sculpture from Indonesia

Little of Javanese and Sumatran buddhist history is available in english. Finding this book, while researching East Javanese jamu origins, was a stroke of luck. Having visited some of the temples and areas listed in the book, Reichle provided the necessary depth my experential travels lacked.

Applied Channel Theory in Chinese Medicine: Wang Ju-Yi's Lectures on Channel Therapeutics

I would not recommend this book but it has its merits. While the author's definition of qi falls far short of the mark and sets the tone for a remedial discussion of therapeutics, snippets of insight and wisdom can be found if willing to endure.

Black Genesis: The Prehistoric Origins of Ancient Egypt

Ever since my 2019 trip to Egypt, I have been endlessly fascinated by the competing historical narratives of this incredible place. Bauval plays an important role as explorer and disruptor in this continually evolving conversation around ancient Egypt. Dismayed to learn the Nabta Playa stones were relocated from their original location to the Aswan Nubia museum.

Curry Cuisine

I constantly search for the recipes that informed my eating experiences while living and travelling in SE Asia. This book does not disappoint. Regional dishes across India, Thailand, Vietnam and Indonesia. It elevated my curry game and I've never looked back. Buy immediately.

Dictionary of the Ben cao gang mu, Volume 1: Chinese Historical Illness Terminology

Being a qualitative science, Traditional Chinese Medicine relies on observational descriptions to capture the essense of a symptom or disease state. Prepare to go deep between the lines of term etymology as pinyin, chinese characters and english translations are examined in referenced discussion. Not a beginner text.

Grey Wolf: The Escape of Adolf Hitler

Loved this book as the standard narrative of Hitler's desmise in a Berlin bunker never met any evidentiary basis that would survive scrutiny in a court of law. Had stumbled upon a similar book some twenty years ago at university. Great to read about some of the locations I have since visited that played a role in the final days of the fleeing Nazi criminal class. Casa Winters in Fuerteventura, for example.

Rita Mulcahay's PMP EXAM PREP 9th edition

As a serial entrepreneur with a solid failure rate, I have found studying the PMP exam prep books helpful in keeping me in my lane and ensuring my organizational workflow is conforming with recognized standards. Reduce complexity, eliminate churn and proceed flexibly step-by-step. A great guide for attaining these goals.

Security Analysis: Sixth Edition

The writings of Benjamin Graham, patron saint of deep value investing, leave me pining for that golden era of investment. Hunting value equities in the overheated bull market has been a fun. Some savants manage it profitably - I have not. This was a gateway book to tackling the CFA curriculum.

The SPARS Pandemic 2025 - 2028: A Futuristic Scenario for Public Health Risk Communicators

If you know, you know. If you don't, you can read it. Weird times are upon us.

The Bright Dark Ages: Comparative and Connective Perspectives (Knowledge Infrastructure and Knowledge Economy)

Came to this book for its discussion on Jamu, the traditional medicine system indigenous to Indonesia. Was not disappointed. Unknown to me was the role Indonesia played as intersectional middle man between India and China during the Buddhist/Hindu heyday. The ramifications of which played no small part in the development of these societies and religions.

The Golden Ratio: The Divine Beauty of Mathematics

Hunting for clues to use the golden ratio in designing a property layout in Indonesia, I came across what is essentially the finest adult coloring book one could wish for. Asked Araujo directly for help on my project. He declined, saying it sounded like a lot of work.

The Noma Guide to Fermentation: Including koji, kombuchas, shoyus, misos, vinegars, garums, lacto-ferments, and black fruits and vegetables

Learn to ferment from these type-A foodie nerds. Necessary reading for my douchi plans.

The Temple in Man: The Secrets of Ancient Egypt

Schwaller's intellectual prowess is next level. I attempt to absorb these chapters with little success but much enjoyment. He is the Manfred Porkert of the Egyptologist crowd. Insightful and beautiful prose.

One-Way Ticket: Nine Lives on Two Wheels

Entertaining read from Vaughters. Was able to contrast his public personality versus the backroom Vaughters I have known: a neo-liberal capitalist leveraging virtue and ethics as marketing assets to promote his for-profit brand. He carved out a niche in European pro biking, toppled Armstrong and perhaps came to accept competitive cycling for the fundamentally broken entity it is. Which makes it great entertainment. I repeat: Entertainment.

The Real Anthony Fauci: Bill Gates, Big Pharma, and the Global War on Democracy and Public Health

Robert Kennedy Jr. is a great human being. The import of this book went unrealized by me until I read its publisher is owned by Simon and Schuster; meaning every claim has been verified by a team of legal experts paid huge money to avoid costly defamation lawsuits. In short, the facts are verified. The emperor wears no clothes.

Batik, Traditional Textiles of Indonesia: From The Rudolf Smend & Donald Harper Collections

A Balinese contact took me to Sidemen in 2017 where we toured a Batik production company housed in an old chicken farm. I had no idea what Batik was. A few years later, at a pan-Indonesian Batik event held at the Solo Palace in Java, I bought as many peices as I could fit into my backpack. Later, my wife and I visited every Batik producer we could find in the Yogyakarta area. Time goes on crutches until I can visit the East Java production houses. This book captures the essence of Batik.

The Sorcerer's Apprentices: A Season in the Kitchen at Ferran Adri√†'s elBulli

Engaging and detailed sneak peak into the world of elBulli, a restaurant I stumbled upon in 2009 while working the Tour of Catalonia. Didn't manage to get a table that day. Ferran's process and methods are revealed here. Suprisingly common place.
